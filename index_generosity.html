Generosity in the Pandemic: Who stayed generous?

The pandemic has pushed millions into poverty. But has the economic strain made people less generous? Depends on the country.

Every year, the Gallup World Poll measures generosity around the world. Each countryâ€™s generosity is calculated not based on how much citizens give, but how many citizens give. Giving can take the form of charitable donations, volunteer time, or helping strangers.

This is 2019 -- before the pandemic.

This is how generosity changed during the pandemic.

In most countries, generosity has gone down relative to average income during the pandemic and stayed low.

Especially countries that started off low in generosity compared to average income, like Greece, Botswana, and Japan.

But a few countries were high in generosity relative to average income and stayed high throughout the pandemic.

Like Indonesia, Myanmar, and Haiti.

A few countries even went up in generosity relative to average income, like Gambia.

While others, like the United States, went down a lot.
